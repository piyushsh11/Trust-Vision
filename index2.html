Why this demo?

See how tiny pixel shifts can flip critical AI decisions.

Trust Vision is an adversarial lab for SDG‑aligned computer vision. We hit models with FGSM, PGD, CW, and AutoAttack, then contrast clean vs. defended outputs using a TRADES/adv‑trained ResNet18 checkpoint when available. Certified L2 radii stay small (~0.02–0.03), highlighting residual risk even after defenses.

SDG 3 (health): retinal/medical images can mis-triage patients if perturbed. SDG 9 (industry): PCB/print/industrial shots can pass defective parts. The goal is to surface how brittle baselines are and what monitoring + CI guardrails you need.

Pick a sample or upload your own, set ε, run an attack, and watch clean → adv → defended outputs with live metrics and drift alerts.

How this maps to SDGs

SDG 3 (Health): Adversarial noise on retinal or radiology images can flip disease/no-disease calls, delaying care. SDG 9 (Industry/Innovation): Vision QC lines can pass faulty parts (e.g., PCB/print defects) or halt production.

In our demo, ε=8/255 attacks succeed frequently and certified radii are small, showing significant residual risk even with light defenses. Live metrics log misclassifications and attack success; alerts fire when the rolling success rate is high—treat this as drift/vulnerability detection. Use the included CI script (robustness_check.py) to fail builds when robust accuracy drops below your threshold.

Backend: FastAPI + PyTorch · Attacks: FGSM / PGD / CW / AutoAttack · Defense: denoise + adv-trained checkpoint ready
